### Understanding Intermittent Tooth Pain

Tooth pain that comes and goes can be frustrating and confusing. Even if the
discomfort disappears for a while, it often signals an underlying dental issue that
needs attention.

### Common Causes of Tooth Pain

Intermittent tooth pain can occur due to [tooth sensitivity](https://www.biolase.com/blog/6-common-causes-of-dental-pain-and-what-to-do-about-it/), early cavities, a cracked
tooth, gum infection, or teeth grinding. Hot or cold foods, sugary snacks, and
pressure while chewing can trigger the pain, making it seem like the problem has
gone away when it actually hasn’t.

### Why You Shouldn’t Ignore It

Temporary relief does not mean the issue is resolved. Dental problems tend to
worsen over time if left untreated. Seeking professional care early can help
prevent complications, reduce discomfort, and protect your natural teeth.
